Feynman Path Integral Evaluation

Read time 10 mins

Date : 08/03/2024

The Feynman Path Integral is a method of calculating the wavefunction of a particle in a potential. The method is based on the principle of least action and is used to calculate the probability amplitude of a particle moving from one point to another in a given time. The path integral is a sum over all possible paths that the particle can take, weighted by the action of each path. The method is used in quantum mechanics to calculate the wavefunction of a particle in a potential and is an important tool in the study of quantum field theory.

Fourier Analysis of Interference Patterns

Read time 10 mins

Date : 21/03/2023

Fourier analysis is a mathematical technique that allows us to decompose a function into its constituent frequencies. It is used in a wide range of applications, from signal processing to image analysis. In this article, we will use Fourier analysis to model the intensity function of an interference pattern in a 4f-optical setup. The 4f-optical setup is a common configuration used in optical systems to filter and process images. By using Fourier analysis, we can gain insight into the behavior of the interference pattern and the effect of spatial filtering on the image.
