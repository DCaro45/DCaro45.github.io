Feynman Path Integral Evaluation

Read time 10 mins

Date : 08/03/2024

The Feynman Path Integral is a method of calculating the wavefunction of a particle in a potential. The method is based on the principle of least action and is used to calculate the probability amplitude of a particle moving from one point to another in a given time. The path integral is a sum over all possible paths that the particle can take, weighted by the action of each path. The method is used in quantum mechanics to calculate the wavefunction of a particle in a potential and is an important tool in the study of quantum field theory.

Planck's constant is a fundamental constant that appears in quantum mechanics and is a measure of the scale of quantum effects. It is named after the physicist Max Planck, who first introduced it in 1900 to explain the spectrum of blackbody radiation. In this article, we will explore how Planck's constant can be determined experimentally through the measurement of Bremsstrahlung radiation. Bremsstrahlung radiation is a type of electromagnetic radiation that is emitted when charged particles are decelerated in a material. By measuring the energy of the emitted radiation, we can determine the value of Planck's constant and verify its importance in quantum mechanics.
